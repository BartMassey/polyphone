TREE


Structure

The tree, located at the left side of the software, provides a global view and a quick access to the elements of all soundfonts under edition.

Each opened .sf2 file is recognized by its title, and contains 3 subtrees:

  • Samples
    All samples are listed under this subtree. A click on one of them leads to the page of sample edition.
  • Instruments
    This tree contains all instruments present in the .sf2 file. Each instrument may contain samples. A click on an instrument or on one of its linked samples leads to the page of instrument edition.
  • Presets
    The presets are gathered here and each preset may contain instruments. A click on a preset or on one of its linked instruments shows the page of preset edition.
A click on the file title, or on the header of one of the three subtrees shows the page of global information edition.

Contextual menu

A right clic on an element shows a menu comprising the following actions:
  • New sample...
    Import new samples.
  • New instrument...
    Create a new instrument.
  • New preset...
    Create a new preset.
  • Bind to...
    Bind the selected sample(s) to an instrument, or the selected instrument(s) to a preset.
  • Replace by...
    Replace a sample linked to an instrument by another sample, or replace an instrument linked to a preset by another instrument. The configuration of the sample or instrument is kept.
  • Delete
    Delete the selected element(s).
  • Rename or bulk rename
    Rename the selected element. Several samples may be simultaneously renamed according to different options:
    • overwrite existing name with rootkey as suffix,
    • overwrite existing name with numerical ascending suffix,
    • append to existing name,
    • prepend to existing name.
  • Close file
    Close the file under edition.

Drag & drop

The operation drag & drop in the tree enables rapid associations or copies within or between .sf2 files.

  • Dragging one or more sample(s)...
    • to an instrument within the same file: association of the sample(s) to the instrument.
    • to another file: copy of the sample(s) to destination file.
  • Dragging one or more instrument(s)...
    • to a preset within the same file: association of the instrument(s) to the preset.
    • to another instrument within the same file: association of the linked samples of the selected instrument(s) to the destination instrument, with the same configuration.
    • to another file: copy of the instrument(s) to the destination file, including all needed samples.
  • Dragging one or more linked sample(s) of one or more instrument(s)...
    • to an instrument within the same file: addition of the linked sample(s) of the selected instrument(s) to the destination instrument, with the same configuration.
  • Dragging one or more preset(s)...
    • to another preset within the same file: association of the linked instruments of the selected preset(s) to the destination preset.
    • to another file: copy of the preset(s) to the destination file, including all needed instruments and samples.
  • Dragging one or more linked instrument(s) of one or more preset(s)...
    • to another preset within the same file: addition of the linked instrument(s) of the selected preset(s) to the destination preset, with the same configuration.

    Note: the operation copy / paste allows the same operations.

    Miscellaneous

    A search bar is available for filtering the elements displayed in the tree. A click on the cross located to the right of the editable field cancels the filter.

    Two buttons located to the left of the editable field provided for search expand or collapse the entire tree.
