Sf2 files allow samples storage along with numerous parameters in order to create instruments driven by MIDI signals and readable by synthetisers using wavetables. These files contain a data structure made of 3 principal elements:

  • samples, coming from a trompet or a piano for instance,
  • instruments, made of samples,
  • presets, made of instruments.
Further to these elements definition and setting, a sf2 file contains also general information, mainly about its origin.

POLYPHONE software has for first goal to create and edit .sf2 files, versions 2.01 and 2.04. It is made of:

  • a menu bar,
  • a toolbar,
  • a left part comprising a tree showing all loaded sf2 files and their different components,
  • a right part allowing the editing of the selected element in the tree.

The right part offers several interfaces allowing the editing of:

  • the general information of the file,
  • the samples,
  • the instruments,
  • the presets.

The second goal of POLYPHONE is to offer tools, accessible through the menu, facilitating and automating the editing of various parameters.
