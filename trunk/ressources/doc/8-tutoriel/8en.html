To create a soundfont, click on "New" in the "File" menu. Soundfonts being made of a 3-level structure, the creation of a new soundfont involves three fundamental steps:

  • sample preparation,
  • instrument creation, comprising samples,
  • preset creation, comprising instruments.

Once the soundfont is created, it is possible to play with the built-in synthesizer (using the virtual keyboard) or use another sf2 compatible software. The format being old and very common, external applications are numerous. Here are some examples:

  • playing using FluidSynth,
  • listening to midi files with TiMidity, WildMIDI or QuickTime,
  • editing and playing sheets with MuseScore.

Hardware such as samplers, soundcards or even synthesizers may also support the sf2 format.
